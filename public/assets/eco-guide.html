Understanding Food Waste

Food waste is a global issue that affects our environment, economy, and society. This guide will help you understand the impact of food waste and provide practical solutions to reduce it.

Environmental Impact

  • Food waste contributes to 8% of global greenhouse gas emissions
  • Wasted food means wasted water, energy, and resources used in production
  • Landfill decomposition of food waste produces methane, a potent greenhouse gas

Smart Shopping Habits

Plan Your Meals: Create a weekly meal plan and shopping list
Check Your Fridge: Take inventory before shopping
Buy Local: Support local farmers and reduce transportation emissions

Storage Solutions

  • Keep fruits and vegetables in proper storage conditions
  • Use airtight containers for leftovers
  • Freeze surplus food before it spoils
  • Understand food date labels (Best Before vs. Use By)

Creative Cooking

  • Use vegetable scraps for homemade broth
  • Transform stale bread into croutons or breadcrumbs
  • Make smoothies with overripe fruits
  • Create new meals from leftovers

Composting Basics

Composting is a natural process that turns organic waste into nutrient-rich soil:

  • Start with a small compost bin or pile
  • Add a mix of green (food scraps) and brown (leaves, paper) materials
  • Keep it moist and turn regularly
  • Use finished compost in your garden

Community Involvement

  • Share surplus food with neighbors
  • Donate to local food banks
  • Participate in community composting programs
  • Join food rescue initiatives
